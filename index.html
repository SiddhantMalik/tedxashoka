Ashoka University

Ashoka University is a philanthropic initiative with a strong emphasis on cross-disciplinary engagement. The university offers programs in a wide range of disciplines, including the humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and computer science. Its curriculum is based on a liberal arts model that emphasizes broad-based learning and critical thinking.

The university has a diverse student body, with students from across India and around the world. The faculty members are acclaimed academics and researchers in their respective fields, with many professors coming from highly-ranked universities.

TEDxAshokaUniversity

TEDxAshokaUniversity is a platform through which we aim to unleash new ideas, inspire and inform. The objective is to promote powerful storytelling, cross-disciplinary interactions, and untapped talents who have the aptitude to create and inspire change. Established in 2015, TEDxAshokaUniversity embodies the passion and spirit of the university.

Gaurav Chintamani

A music producer, audio engineer, musician, and educator, Gaurav has been immersed in the world of sound since 2002. As the founder of Quarter Note Studios, he has composed and produced music for jingles, films, documentaries, theatre, and podcasts, collaborating with a diverse range of artists and commercial clients.

Anubhav Jain

A Goldman Sachs investment banker to a prominent sports commentator is a testament to reinvention. After his H-1B visa application was denied, he pivoted from a high-powered finance career to sports storytelling, joining ESPNcricinfo during the 2019 Cricket World Cup. With no formal media training, he quickly became a leading voice in cricket, working with networks like Star Sports, Sony Sports, and Cricbuzz, covering major events like the IPL and ICC World Cups. His story highlights the power of following one's passion and embracing unexpected opportunities.

Anusmita Bhattacharyya

She has performed in numerous concerts from the age of 9 with a Western chamber orchestra and has attained certifications from the Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music (ABRSM). She is also a non-fiction writer having headed the journalism department of Redefy Kolkata, an organisation run by teens for teens. Her journey with bipolar disorder has motivated her to talk about mental health among a wider audience.

Ishan Shanavas

Ishan’s book - The Light of Wilder Things - traces his journey exploring India’s wild landscapes as a teenager. He has authored two wildlife field guides, and has contributed to publications like Sanctuary Asia, Nature in Focus, RoundGlass Sustain, and Wildlife SOS. He writes regularly on his website and his weekly newsletter—The Owlet

Pubarun Basu

As the first Indian photographer to win the Sony Youth Photographer of the Year Award and a Forbes 30U30 honoree, Pubarun Basu has conceptualised ideas such as the Dhara and mobilised over 500+ underprivileged youth to stand for environmental conservation. His work has received recognition from UNESCO, BBC, CNN and more.
